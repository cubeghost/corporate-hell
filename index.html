info

Once upon a time, as you may have heard, there was no such thing as border-radius — we had to round our corners by hand. IE6 compatibility was still a genuine and troublesome concern, and we had no Mozilla Developer Network to save us from the throes of W3C Schools. These were dark times.

I learned HTML from Neopets when it was in its prime and picked up CSS somewhere along the way, then jumped headfirst into Javascript by fiddling with copied and pasted code snippets. I started working with PHP when I was 13. My whole life has been spent around computers, but at the same time it's been spent around art — from primitive drawings on the white walls of my parents' house to the glitch art I continue to refine today. Aesthetics are just as important to me as functionality, so I craft things with both in mind.

Beautiful and artful front-end experiences are what I love to build most.

I grew up in suburban Detroit with a programmer and graphic designer for parents. After discovering that traditional higher education is totally not for me, I now live in Brooklyn, doing full-time web development work for Girl Scouts of the USA. As someone who is both queer and disabled, activism and human rights work are important parts of my life, and I am currently doing volunteer work for a nonprofit focused on ending police brutality. I like rocks, maps, plants, and enjoying life as a gay, genderless space alien.
